Intro

We're just sketching this out, but the general idea behind Pythoneers.Club, is that coders work asynchronously, remotely and on so many different platforms, solving so many different kinds of problems, that it can be difficult to learn how or where to ask each other for help. It's especially hard to know how to isolate the relevant part of your code you're struggling with to share with others. And then how do you share it? Sometimes you just know there's a way to do something, but you don't know what words to use to describe it. And even then, who are you going to ask?

The good news is that it is extremely rare to find a problem in programming someone else hasn't already solved. The answers are out there, and the Python community is particularly well-known for being incredibly welcoming, generous and helpful.

So Pythoneers.Club is here to introduce Middle and High School students to that community! We'll steer you towards great online tutorials, books and videos like CodeAcademy, Arcade, the Mu Editor, Code Combat, Check.io, great books like Python for Everybody and Automate the Boring Stuff with Python, hysterical but really substantive video tutorials like Socratica, and so much more! (Watch this space for a tag-cloud of recommended resources.)

But, rather than try to teach or work together through any particular curriculum, we're going to focus on helping you choose you own, and then giving you somewhere to go when you need help.
